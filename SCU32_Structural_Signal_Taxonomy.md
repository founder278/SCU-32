# SCU•32 Structural Signal Taxonomy  
### Operational Signal Classification for Institutional Systems

## Purpose

The Structural Signal Taxonomy defines the observable categories of signals that reveal emerging structural stress within institutional systems.

These signals constitute the input layer of the SCU•32 institutional nervous system and are processed primarily by OBSERVA•32 and DIAGNOSTIC•32.

Structural signals generally appear before visible systemic failure and therefore function as early indicators of institutional drift.

---

## Signal Category 1 — Friction Signals

Friction signals reflect increasing resistance within coordination processes.

Examples include:

- increasing meeting density without decision output  
- rising communication overhead  
- growing administrative burden  
- repeated rework cycles  
- delayed cross-department coordination  

Friction signals typically represent early coordination inefficiencies and may precede structural drift.

---

## Signal Category 2 — Drift Signals

Drift signals reflect gradual divergence between institutional objectives and operational outcomes.

Examples include:

- decision-outcome misalignment  
- policy goals failing to produce expected results  
- persistent deviation between planning and execution  
- metric deterioration despite corrective efforts  

Drift signals indicate weakening alignment between institutional structure and system behavior.

---

## Signal Category 3 — Saturation Signals

Saturation signals emerge when institutional capacity approaches operational limits.

Examples include:

- decision backlog growth  
- accumulation of unresolved issues  
- increasing processing delays  
- resource bottlenecks  

Saturation signals often precede increases in Correction Latency.

---

## Signal Category 4 — Authority Conflict Signals

Authority conflict signals arise when governance structures produce overlapping or contradictory decision pathways.

Examples include:

- contradictory decisions from leadership layers  
- unclear decision authority  
- overlapping jurisdiction between departments  
- policy conflict between institutional bodies  

Authority conflict signals frequently increase Decision Latency and governance instability.

---

## Signal Category 5 — Coordination Failure Signals

Coordination failure signals reflect breakdowns in information flow or operational synchronization.

Examples include:

- projects failing due to communication gaps  
- teams operating with incompatible assumptions  
- fragmented decision processes  
- loss of synchronization between system components  

Coordination failure signals increase both Error Accumulation Rate and Correction Latency.

---

## Signal Category 6 — Signal Distortion Signals

Signal distortion occurs when the information reaching decision layers becomes inaccurate, delayed, or filtered.

Examples include:

- reporting bias  
- metric manipulation  
- delayed incident reporting  
- information filtering across hierarchical layers  

Signal distortion weakens the sensing capacity of OBSERVA•32 and delays system correction.

---

## Signal Category 7 — Structural Stress Signals

Structural stress signals appear when the system approaches its Stability Tolerance Threshold.

Examples include:

- rapid escalation of unresolved issues  
- persistent institutional conflict  
- resource exhaustion  
- loss of operational reliability  

These signals often indicate proximity to systemic instability.

---

## Signal Severity Levels

Structural signals may appear at different stages of system evolution.

### Early Signals
- weak friction  
- small deviations  
- emerging inefficiencies  

### Escalating Signals
- visible drift  
- coordination breakdown  
- growing backlog  

### Critical Signals
- system saturation  
- governance conflict  
- structural stress  

---

## Integration with SCU•32 Engines

OBSERVA•32 detects and monitors emerging structural signals.

DIAGNOSTIC•32 interprets signals and identifies their structural causes.

COHERENCE•32 evaluates the implications of detected signals for system stability.

CONTROL•32 determines whether corrective intervention is required.

ACTUA•32 executes interventions designed to restore institutional coherence.

---

## Conclusion

The Structural Signal Taxonomy provides the observational foundation required for estimating Correction Latency and Error Accumulation Rate.

By detecting structural signals early, institutional systems can reduce correction latency and maintain stability within their tolerance thresholds.

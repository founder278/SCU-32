## Overview

SCU•32 proposes a structural approach to artificial intelligence governance inspired by biological nervous systems and control theory.

Instead of focusing only on model capability, the framework focuses on maintaining **system coherence under pressure**.

Modern institutions operate as complex adaptive systems.  
When coherence is lost, systems experience drift, friction, and structural instability.

SCU•32 introduces an architecture capable of detecting, measuring, and correcting these deviations.

---

## Core Principle

Coherence is not a philosophical property.

It is a **system constraint**.

When coherence degrades, organizations experience:

• decision-outcome drift  
• structural friction  
• correction energy spikes  
• coordination breakdown  

SCU•32 provides mechanisms to monitor and regulate these dynamics.

# Institutional Artificial Nervous Systems
## A Coherence-Based Architecture for Adaptive Governance

### Abstract

Modern institutions increasingly operate as complex adaptive systems where decisions propagate through interconnected technological, human, and economic layers. Traditional governance structures often fail to detect systemic drift until failures become visible.

This research note introduces the SCU•32 Framework, a coherence-based architecture inspired by biological nervous systems and control theory. The framework proposes a structural layer capable of observing system conditions, evaluating coherence between decisions and outcomes, and enabling adaptive governance responses.

The objective is to maintain system viability under stress and prevent structural fragmentation before systemic failure occurs.

---

### The Problem

Many institutional failures are not caused by single incorrect decisions but by the gradual loss of structural coherence within the system.

Common symptoms include:

- organizational burnout
- policy drift
- delayed corrective signals
- incentive misalignment
- structural friction between decision layers

These failures emerge when feedback loops inside the system weaken.

---

### Biological Inspiration

Biological nervous systems maintain coherence through three continuous functions:

Observation  
Evaluation  
Correction

SCU•32 applies this structural logic to institutional systems.

---

### Architectural Layers

The framework proposes four interacting components:

OBSERVA•32 – system sensing and observability layer  
DIAGNOSTIC•32 – structural analysis of decisions and system conditions  
COHERENCE•32 – detection of drift and structural friction  
CONTROL•32 – adaptive governance control plane

Together these layers form an Institutional Artificial Nervous System.

---

### Operational Loop

Observation → Evaluation → Coherence Analysis → Adaptive Intervention

This loop allows institutions to detect structural instability before failure occurs.

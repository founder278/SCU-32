# Institutional Artificial Nervous Systems

A Neuro-Inspired Architecture for Adaptive Governance

Author: Amilcar Oliva  
Organization: CU Core Technologies LLC  
Domain: Intelligent Systems Architecture  

---

## Abstract

Modern institutions operate as complex adaptive systems exposed to rapid technological, social, and environmental change. Traditional governance structures often lack the sensing, feedback, and adaptation mechanisms necessary to maintain structural coherence under dynamic conditions.

This paper proposes the concept of **Institutional Artificial Nervous Systems (IANS)** — an architectural framework that models institutional intelligence using principles inspired by biological nervous systems.

Within this architecture, artificial intelligence operates not merely as a generator of outputs but as a **regulatory layer that senses system conditions, evaluates structural coherence, and enables adaptive governance.**

---

## Core Architecture

The SCU•32 framework structures institutional intelligence through several coordinated layers.

### CONTROL•32  
The governance control plane responsible for rule enforcement and system stability.

### COHERENCE•32  
A structural evaluation engine measuring system coherence and detecting drift between decisions and outcomes.

### OBSERVA•32  
The system observability layer that continuously monitors system signals and operational indicators.

### DIAGNOSTIC•32  
An analytical engine designed to detect organizational stress and structural dysfunction.

Together these components operate as the **core nervous system** of the institutional architecture.

---

## Institutional Decision Layer

Information collected through observation and diagnostic processes feeds into the institutional decision layer.

This layer enables adaptive governance mechanisms capable of responding to emerging structural conditions.

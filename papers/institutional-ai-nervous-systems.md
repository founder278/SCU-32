# Institutional Artificial Nervous Systems

## A Coherence-Based Architecture for Adaptive Governance

Modern institutions operate as complex adaptive systems exposed to continuous pressure, uncertainty, and structural drift.

Traditional artificial intelligence systems focus primarily on prediction or generation of outputs. However, they rarely address the deeper challenge faced by institutions: maintaining **structural coherence under pressure**.

SCU•32 introduces a new architectural paradigm: **Institutional Artificial Nervous Systems**.

In this model, artificial intelligence functions not only as a generator of information, but as a **regulatory nervous system for institutions**.

The architecture integrates four core engines:

CONTROL•32  
Coherence Control Plane governing authority, intervention, rollback, and decision integrity.

COHERENCE•32  
Structural coherence evaluation engine measuring system drift, friction, correction energy, and alignment.

OBSERVA•32  
System observability layer capturing signals, metrics, and structural anomalies across the institution.

DIAGNOSTIC•32  
Organizational stress detection engine identifying early signs of structural breakdown.

Together, these engines allow institutions to detect, measure, and correct coherence degradation before systemic failure occurs.

This architecture proposes that **coherence is not a philosophical concept but a viability constraint for complex systems**.

Under this framework, artificial intelligence becomes an institutional nervous system capable of monitoring structural health and supporting adaptive governance under pressure.

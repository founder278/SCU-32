# SCU•32 — System Architecture

## Institutional Artificial Nervous Systems

SCU•32 models institutions as adaptive systems capable of detecting structural stress, interpreting signals, evaluating coherence, producing governance decisions, and executing corrective interventions.

The architecture is inspired by biological nervous systems and treats governance as a continuous feedback process rather than a static decision structure.

Instead of reacting only after failure occurs, the system continuously evaluates structural signals and maintains institutions within their operational stability domain.
---

## Core Architectural Principle

Complex institutions tend to fail when three structural conditions degrade:

- coherence  
- boundaries  
- flow control  

SCU•32 is designed to continuously monitor and regulate these conditions through a closed-loop governance architecture.
